My Culture

Family is the most important thing that both Ghanaians and Jamaicans value. My dad's side, taken right before the 10 year anniversary of my grand-fathers passing. The elders in my family are a strong influence on my actions and morals

Growing up in a somewhat "mixed background" household, my parents have always stood by and taken what they value most in their cultures and made them core values in our household. Birthday celebrations were one of those things.

This is the place I identify as my hometown, North Attleboro, Massachusetts. I lived here until the age of 6, and even though I was very young, I swear I will always remember this place

As a Boston native, I'm a MASSIVE New England Patriots fan. It's something that I very strongly identify as, especially when sports comes up as a topic. This is one of the few topics I'm closed to when it comes to opening up to other teams

My parents instilled in me a interest in sports at a young age, as their parents did when they were children. I still love sports to this day and can get pretty conflicted when Ghana and Jamaica have a rare Olympic meeting.

Music is something I consider to be one of the biggest aspects of my life. Coming from two cultures where music is seen as the soundtrack to everyday life, I often see music as my passion. J-Cole exemplifies that passion for me

My favourite meal is butter chicken. In a way, it blends my mother's and father's culture, as curry chicken is popular among Jamaicans, but the modified African sauce my father cooks it in is what give its unique touch

This is a photo of me as a newborn. Even though I've grown up quite a bit since then, I still identify as, and consider myself, a kid at heart!

I'd say this comes from my Jamaican side more than Ghanaian, but my mom really instilled in me the importance of social gatherings and just having fun with friends whenever you can, its one of the few things we can control in life

Once again, my friends are the ones who have also helped me grow and learn about who I am, so it's only right that they get the credit that they deserve as well. To me, my friends are my family too, and family is for life!

I took this picture during the closing moments of a Drake concert in MTL (LOVE concerts), and I think it really identifies me in a way. Canada has been my footing of sorts, the background to my canvas of cultures and countries I've been exposed to
